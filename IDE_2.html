How has the weather changed over the past 100 years in Bucharest

The plot above is a scatter plot showing the weather changes (taking into account the meteorological mean temperatures) over the past 100 years in Bucharest.
Note that we don't have values for 1976 and 1980 due to external factors (these points can be seen up in the chart, representing outliers - the values in the data
were 999).
Our motivation in choosing a scatter plot is that we work with continuous data (the mean temperatures over a period of time). Scatter plots are mostly used to indicate
trends or correlations between dimensions. We want to see the trend of the temperatures during a period of time and there is an increase happenning from '90s on.
The graph might be improved by adding a line between the points to represent better the evolution of the data (the x axis data is order so it would not be an inconvenient).
The data set consists in a csv file containing a column with the mean annual temperature and another one with the year for which we analyze the weather.
From the resulting visualization we can conclude that the temperatures in Bucharest differs from one year to another, in the early '30s and '40s
evolving from 8 to 11,5 degrees, but remaining between these values until '90s. We can notice an increasing temperature from '90s until present
(values between 9 and 12 comparing with lower values in the past).
